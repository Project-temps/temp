About Us

ET4D TEAM

ET4D consists of 11 participants from 8 different countries - 6 EU countries and 2 non-EU countries

The participating partners are: ATB Leibniz Institute for Agricultural Engineering and Bioeconomy (DE), AgHiTech Ltd (HU), Innvite (DK), Estonian University of Life Sciences (EE), MIGAL Galilee Technology Center (IL), University of Zielona Góra (PL), Tokat Gaziosmanpaşa Üniversitesi (TR), AKI Institue of Agricultural Economics (HU), Aarhus University (DK), University of Oulu (FIN), and Wroclaw University of Environmental and Life Sciences (PL).

ET4D connects companies, academic research institutions and universities with a geographical coverage that supports the reinforcement of association and economic integration of countries with different socio-economic preconditions.

ET4D involves partners from multiple disciplines, including agricultural, environmental, mechanical, computer and communication engineering as well as physics, economics, sociology and political science. This mix permits to address the complexity of information transfer from farms way down to the consumer or other interest groups from multiple perspectives.

Work Packages and Timeline

The ET4D work program covers three years and is organised in six work packages (WP), each associated with two to four respective milestones and deliverables. The first WP deals with communication needs of agri-food systems in general and the assessment and enhancement of on-farm ICT infrastructure in selected case study facilities. The second WP focusses on on-farm sensor data collection. It involves the validation and demonstration of a semi-commercial environmental monitoring system for barns as well as the exploration and assessment of further simplified monitoring systems. The third WP deals with the diagnosis of information needs of selected primary target groups for the dairy sector and of potential concerns of farmers related to data privacy and sharing in order to define suitable levels of data aggregation for interest group tailored reports. The forth WP conflates specific data from WP2 and WP3 and literature, to provide an online information and decision support application for different interest groups in the dairy sector. The fifth WP concerns the strategic planning and evaluation of the dissemination, communication, exploitation and networking activities to maximize the outreach of the project. Finally, the sixth WP is dedicated to internal project communication and management, to promote efficient workflows and timely delivery of results.

  • WP1: Dependable Wireless Connectivity for Farms
    • 1. Assess the communication key performance indicator (KPI) requirements and traffic patterns of agri-food embedded sensors and DMS.
    • 2. Characterize the state-of-the-art connectivity performance at test areas.
    • 3. Suggest methods to improve connectivity for DMS and the agri-food sector.
  • WP2: Experimentation Sites
    • 1. Validate a low-cost environmental monitoring system.
    • 2. Identify and implement interfaces to other sensors used on-farm.
    • 3. Demonstrate scalability and operability of the data management system with embedded sensors.
  • WP3: Diagnosis of Business and Consumer Behavior Influencing Factors
    • 1. Identify factors influencing consumer behavior in the dairy market.
    • 2. Characterize key determinants of business/purchasing decisions.
    • 3. Characterize information needs of selected target groups for making informed choices.
  • WP4: Data Aggregation and Management
    • 1. Data processing and implementation of basic empirical models.
    • 2. Definition and implementation of information aggregation and assessment schemes.
    • 3. Designing, coding, and testing of a modular web application.
  • WP5: Dissemination, Exploitation, and Communication
    • 1. Design and execute a strategy to disseminate and exploit project results.
    • 2. Create awareness of project findings and results, and of the economic and environmental potential of on-farm data management and inter-interest-group specific reporting.
    • 3. Enable interest groups to use the results for value creation.
    • 4. Gather and document all innovation in the project to encourage future research and spin-off ideas.
  • WP6: Project Coordination
    • 1. Foster efficient information flow.
    • 2. Early identification, assessment, and management of administrative and technical risks.
    • 3. Interaction with ICT-AGRI-FOOD.
